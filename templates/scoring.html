How does it work?

Our team rate each brand from 1-5 at each of the lifecycle stages. We then calculate the average of these numbers to find the total score. Each score is then rounded to the nearest whole number.

Our ratings are:

5 Excellent

Very high level of supply chain transparency. Excellent ethical and environmental standards.

4 Good

High level of supply chain transparency. Good ethical and environmental standards.

3 Average

Some effort towards supply chain transparency. Room for improvement in ethical and environmental standards.

2 Unsatisfactory

Insufficient evidence found. Poor ethical and environmental standards.

1 Poor

No evidence found. Very poor ethical and environmental standards.

Our ratings are based primarily on public information though we endeavor to work with brands to fill any gaps. We provide scores for brands as a whole rather than individual products, so results may vary. We also score using the best of our researcher's knowledge at the time of writing but scores will be subject to future review and updates.

Criteria

DesignThis is the inception stage where materials, processes and methods can be changed to allow for upstream improvements.
Raw MaterialsThis is the stage in which raw materials for footwear are produced. Typical raw materials include leather (animal and non-animal origin), textiles (non-animal origin, natural and synthetic and animal origin), rubber, plastics, metal, wood, recycled materials and waste-origin materials.
Material ManufacturingThis is the stage where raw materials are manufactured into the processed materials that will be directly used in the production of footwear. This stage includes leather (animal origin), textile (synthetic and natural, animal and non-animal origin), rubber, plastic, wood and metal manufacturing.
Footwear ManufacturingAt this stage, the materials are assembled into a finished footwear product. This is considered to be the brand level and/or under their immediate control.
RetailThe retail stage includes logistics, sales, marketing and CRM.
UseThis stage includes everything the customer does with a product during its useful life, including wear, care and repair.
DisposalThe final stage is disposal, where a customer may be able to dispose of a product by recycling, reselling etc.

Blurb

**Browse beyond size and style** Transparency is crucial when it comes to ethical and sustainable business. We know that trying to find a shoe that aligns with your values can be frustrating and time consuming. With so much jargon, greenwashing, and information it can take you hours to wade through and compare brands. And when you finally order that perfect pair you panic over whether you’ve made the right choice after all. Trust us, we’ve been on those 3am spirals too. Knowing this, we created our ethics and sustainability framework to provide accessible information on brand standards to help you make informed purchase decisions. And so that all the information you need is finally in one place. Now you can browse and compare shoes based not only on size and style but by their sustainability efforts too! Our framework is constantly evolving and we review and update it regularly to maintain the ease and accuracy of finding the right pair of sustainable shoes for you. Currently, our framework doesn’t extend to our refurbished second-hand shoes but we are working on it!

Notice
